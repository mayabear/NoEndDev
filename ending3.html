Food Production



The Uk collectively thows away 13 billion tons of food each year, food which is often sourced from further away isntead of locally. Furthermore, the meat we consume and throw away high ecological impacts, with meat such as beef needing 160x more land than staples like potatoes and rice, as well as producing 11x more greenhouse gases. Here are some actions regarding food consumption you can take if you want to be more sustainable!


Get Involved!

Initiatives in St. Andrews


  1. Vegetarian society

    The vegetarian hosts weekly pot-lucks or bonfires on Sundays. Joining is an easy, fun, and cheap way to reduce your carbon footprint, since you're eating vegetables instead of meat! Find when/where their next potluck is on their Facebook

  2. The tree

    The Tree is an iniative by Transition St. Andrews that aims to support local businesses through selling their local organic produce in St. Andrews. You order organic food through their Website. and pick it up at their pop-up shops!

  3. Edible Campus

    The edible campus aims, another Transition project, reinvigorates our interest in eating more locally. By increasing the amount produced in the university, we can reduce our carbon footprint. Go wander around campus to see the edible campusses, and feel free to take anything you like!

What you can do yourself!


  1. Buy locally sourced food

    Food bought locally does not travel as far, thereby having a lower Carbon footprint! St. Andrews has some good farmers market where you can buy locally sourced produce.

  2. Reduce your meat intake

    Food bought locally does not travel as far, thereby reducing its Carbon footprint

    Previous Next
  3. Think ahead and prepare your meals

    Think ahead of what meals you will eat this week, and buy the food accordingly. This ensures no food goes to waste!
